Circuit Breaker Trips

This condition may be related to the precipitator or to the electrical control system. It will definitely affect the performance of the Precipitator since any field not operating correctly will have impact on the outlet emissions. Indications are as follows:

  • The TR control will not keep the ESP field energized.
  • Consistently shows low voltage with surges of high current.
  • TR cabinet / MCC circuit breaker trips frequently.
Possible causes of the above events are as follows:
  • AVC or firing circuit malfunction.
  • Faulty circuit breaker.
  • SCR shorted or misfiring.
  • Incorrect primary current signal.
  • Incorrect "Phasing‚Äù of the SCRs.

COURSE OF ACTION:

If the microprocessor control and SCR pack are operating properly the AVC will de-energize the control and display an over current trip message without tripping the breaker. Failure of the AVC to do this requires the following steps to be taken:

  • Replace the AVC and or Firing circuit - Retest
  • To eliminate a short to ground in the control cabinet as the problem, disconnect the field wiring going to the primary of the transformer rectifier. Connect four 110 volt incandescent lamps in series across the output of the cabinet. Since the cabinet no longer has a substantial load, re-energizing the cabinet should produce line voltage and very very low current. The light bulbs will go to full brightness and measuring the voltage across them will show the full mains voltage. If the breaker trips there is a ground fault in the cabinet.
  • Performing the light bulb test and ensuring the voltage across the ligh bulbs rises smoothly to the mains voltage will verify the connections to the SCR gate / cathodes is correct. While on the light bulb load, the breaker will not trip, but intermittent flashing of the light bulbs or spurious output to them would indicate incorrect connections to the SCR gate / cathodes. If a TR load were connected at this time, there is a high probability of tripping the circuit breaker, blowing a line fuse and even damaging the SCR pack.
  • If after performing all the above checks it is determined that all components are in proper working order yet the breaker continues to trip, replace the breaker. As a double check, place a recording amp meter on the line feed between the breaker and the contactor. If the current does not exceed the control rating, yet the breaker keeps tripping, the breaker is definitely defective.
