Under Voltage Trip (low or no primary or secondary voltage, high or rated primary or secondary current)

Or

No Primary or Secondary Voltage and High or Rated Primary or Secondary Current

Or

Under Voltage Trip

This condition is most likely to be related to the precipitator. It will definitely affect the performance of the Precipitator since any field not operating correctly will have impact on the outlet emissions. Indications are as follows:

  • The TR control will not keep the ESP field energized.
  • Consistently shows low voltage and may exhibit surges of high current.
  • AVC displays an Under Voltage trip condition.
Possible causes of the above events are as follows:
  • Full Hoppers.
  • Cracked or Tracking Insulator.
  • Broken or swinging discharge electrode.
  • Excessive dust build up on either Discharge Electrodes or Collector Plates.
  • Bridging in hopper.
  • Dust build up on Anti-sway Insulators.

COURSE OF ACTION:

If the primary voltage drops below the setting of the Under Voltage trip limit the AVC will trip off (there may be a time delay). If this occurs, the AVC should display an Under Voltage trip condition. To isolate the problem, disconnect the precipitator load from the high voltage transformer rectifier (TR) and re-energize. If primary and secondary voltage is now present (there should be no primary or secondary current) the ground is located within the precipitator. If the voltage is still low or zero the problem is within the TR tank. For problems within the TR tank consult the manufacture of the TR. For problems within the precipitator perform a complete internal inspection.
