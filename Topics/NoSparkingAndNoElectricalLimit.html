No Sparking is Occurring and No Electrical Limit is Being Reached

This condition will definitely affect the performance of the Precipitator since any field not operating correctly will have impact on the outlet emissions. In an instance where: The control is not under any type of Energy Management, not in manual mode, and there is no sparking or arcing, at least one of the electrical parameters should be at a limit. This limit could be one of the ratings of the transformer, or it could be a lower value programmed into the control. Regardless of what the limit is, some limit should be reached. Possible causes of the above events are as follows:

  • The AVC is probably defective.
  • The CLR is incorrectly sized
  • The CLR is in the process of failing.

COURSE OF ACTION:

  • Check the conduction angle of the SCRs on the AVC. If it does not show full conduction, replace the AVC with a known working unit.
  • Contact us with the CLR current rating, mHy(millihenry) value(of available taps) and your TR ratings as well as operational readings and we will calculate whether your CLR is correctly sized
  • Replace the CLR with a known good unit.
